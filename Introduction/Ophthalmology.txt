If a patient has a visual acuity of 6/12, what does this mean?
A) They can see at 6 meters what a person with normal vision can see at 12 meters
B) They can see at 12 meters what a person with normal vision can see at 6 meters
C) Their vision is better than normal, resolving details invisible to the average eye at 6 meters
D) They have normal visual acuity equivalent to 6/6
E) They can only perceive hand movements at a distance of 6 meters
Correct Answer: A) They can see at 6 meters what a person with normal vision can see at 12 meters
Explanation:
6/12 = test distance/normal distance, meaning reduced acuity, not enhanced. B reverses the fraction (would mean better-than-normal vision), C and D wrongly describe normal or supranormal vision, and E describes near-blindness, far worse than 6/12.

What condition causes acute painful loss of vision?
A) Central retinal artery occlusion
B) Acute angle-closure glaucoma
C) Cataract
D) Age-related macular degeneration
E) Retinal detachment
Correct Answer: B) Acute angle-closure glaucoma
Explanation:
Only angle-closure glaucoma is both acute and painful. A and E are acute but painless, while C and D are painless and gradual, not acute.

What is the term for pupils of different sizes?
A) Nystagmus
B) Anisocoria
C) Ptosis
D) Diplopia
E) RAPD
Correct Answer: B) Anisocoria
Explanation:
Anisocoria specifically means unequal pupil size. Nystagmus is involuntary eye movement, ptosis is lid drooping, diplopia is double vision, and RAPD is an abnormal light reflex — none describe pupil size difference.

What is the visual field exam called?
A) Ishihara test
B) Swinging light test
C) Confrontation test
D) Snellen test
E) Ophthalmoscopy
Correct Answer: C) Confrontation test
Explanation:
The confrontation test is the bedside visual field exam. A tests color, B tests for RAPD, D tests acuity, and E examines the fundus — all test different functions.

What are the three components involved in accommodation?
A) Accommodation, pupillary constriction, and convergence
B) Accommodation, pupillary constriction, and divergence
C) Accommodation, pupillary dilation, and divergence
D) Accommodation, pupillary dilation, and convergence
E) Convergence, dilation, and visual acuity
Correct Answer: A) Accommodation, pupillary constriction, and convergence
Explanation:
The near reflex triad is lens accommodation + pupillary constriction + convergence. B and C swap in divergence (the opposite of convergence), D swaps in dilation (opposite of constriction), and E replaces accommodation with visual acuity, which isn't part of the triad.

The limbus houses germinative stem cells that maintain the corneal epithelium. What is the primary function of this layer?
A) Anchoring the epithelium via desmosomes only
B) Maintaining endothelial cell density
C) Providing mechanical strength via collagen fibrils
D) Regulating aqueous humor outflow
E) Regeneration and proliferation of epithelial cells
Correct Answer: E) Regeneration and proliferation of epithelial cells
Explanation:
Limbal stem cells continuously regenerate the corneal epithelium, allowing rapid repair after abrasion by cell spreading and proliferation. A is incomplete, as anchoring also involves hemidesmosomes and fibrils, not desmosomes alone. B is wrong — the endothelium instead repairs by cell spreading alone with loss of density, no regeneration. C and D describe Bowman's layer and the trabecular meshwork, unrelated structures.
